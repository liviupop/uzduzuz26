Nine fundamentals

principles as working habits

They are not rules we set out to obey, but tendencies our work has revealed about itself: a compact framework for deciding what we do and what we refuse.

01Sustainability through beauty

We make things worth keeping: well-designed objects, careful materials, and projects that can be revisited rather than consumed once.

02Calm technologies

Technology belongs in the toolbox. It should serve the work quietly, without turning attention itself into the product.

03Education for the whole person

We design for body, memory, hand, moral sense, aesthetic faculty, and unanswered questions, not just for skills transfer.

04Active citizenship

Civic practice happens inside ordinary activities: how groups decide, listen, share space, and make things together.

05Explicitly pro-European

European cooperation is not a flavour of our work; it is the architecture: partners, budgets, methods, and public commitments.

06Critical thinking

Critical thinking is a discipline: the patience to test our own first answers, our tools, and the language we use.

07Curiosity and experiment

We protect the right to follow a question without knowing the answer in advance, and we count learning as an outcome.

08Critical technology

We ask what tools do to people, institutions, and democracy, and we use recent technology from inside practice, not at a distance.

09Living heritage

Heritage remains alive when it is used: craft, folk astronomy, cuisine, oral history, and local memory put back into circulation.

Methodology and framework

heritage-led future literacy

Our framework combines non-formal education, ethnographic listening, STEAM, storytelling, future literacy, and critical technology. It is child-centric and participant-led, drawing from Korczak, Montessori, community mapping, nonviolent communication, TEAL learning, and iterative workshop design.

01Observefieldwork, youth needs assessment, cultural and local context
02Collectstories, craft practices, archives, questions, images, tools
03Translatefrom archive to workshop, from idea to object, from heritage to action
04Prototypehands-on making, analog devices, digital tools, AI as thinking aid
05Sharepublic moments, exhibitions, toolkits, peer-to-peer learning
06Reflectevaluation, social choreography, learning outcomes, next iteration

Twelve 3D-printed objects designed with Midjourney and finished by hand. A workshop on future imagination, critical AI use, retrofuturism, solarpunk, and what a community judges worth preserving.
