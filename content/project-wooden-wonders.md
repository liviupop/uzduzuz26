September 2025. International youth exchange on traditional crafts and green tourism. Romanian edition. Thirteen participants from five-plus European countries. Overall score 9.08/10.

## what it was

An international youth exchange focused on traditional wood-craft skills and cultural exchange, bringing European young people to explore Romanian heritage while developing practical skills. Coordinated by [4 Elements](https://uzinaduzina.org/parteneri/4-elementa) (Croatia) as lead, with uzinaduzina as host for the Romanian edition.

## why it mattered

For us, *Wooden Wonders* is the model of what **living heritage** means, in the sense of the principle in our manifesto. Heritage is not a museum; it is a set of techniques and ways of doing that still produce objects. A young Dutch participant learning in three days to carve wood with a Romanian craftsperson is not studying the past; they are entering it as you enter an open workshop.

The combination of material heritage (the techniques), intangible heritage (the language, the gestures, the stories), and European values (mobility, exchange, mutual understanding) means that the project simultaneously embodies three of our founding principles.

## what we did

- **International youth participants** from five-plus European countries.
- **Romanian traditional techniques** of wood processing and crafts.
- **Cultural-exchange and language activities.**
- **Sustainable tourism** and environmental awareness.
- **Youth entrepreneurship** in heritage sectors.
- **Official Youthpass certification** for participants.
- **Full Erasmus+ funding.**

## what we learned

From the (well-documented) feedback:

- **77% rated 9/10 or higher**, indicating exceptional satisfaction.
- **Day three (hands-on workshop) was the most popular**: confirmation that practical activities consistently beat lectures.
- **100% interest in future participation**: a perfect retention indicator.
- **Group dynamics** were consistently praised across feedback.
- Food: 4.3/5 (the only area with concrete room for improvement).

The general lesson: let us **document the group-dynamics facilitation techniques** that worked here, so we can replicate them at other events.
