Project manager and educational resources developer at uzinaduzina. Specialist in Erasmus+ KA220 youth and adult partnerships, cultural mediation for young audiences, and communication strategy for civil society organisations.

## role at uzinaduzina

Mihaela manages the design and delivery of educational materials across uzinaduzina's larger Erasmus+ partnerships, with a particular focus on facilitation guides, learning activities, and participant resources. She also coordinates communication strategy and community building for cultural projects, and co-designs workshops and youth camp programmes that combine hands-on exploration with critical thinking and creative methodology.

## background

Mihaela has worked at the intersection of education, culture, and civil society for more than fifteen years, with formative roles at the Rațiu Centre for Democracy in Turda (2012–2019, returning as consultant in 2025), Transilvania IT Cluster, and several heritage micro-branding initiatives in rural Romania. Her career as PR coordinator and programme manager included the Ion Rațiu Investigative Journalism Award, the Elisabeth Pilkington Mental Health Award, and the Ion Rațiu Democracy Award delivered in partnership with the Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars in Washington and the Rațiu Family Charitable Foundation in London.

In parallel, she has worked as a copywriter for the digital publishing platform PublishDrive and for Centrul Cultural Clujean, producing over a hundred articles on the publishing industry and cultural programmes.

She holds an MA in Communication and Philosophy and a BA in Philosophy and Social Communication, both from Babeș-Bolyai University in Cluj-Napoca. Her academic work focused on body and performance in *art brut* and outsider art traditions. She has translated into Romanian works by Hannah Arendt (*The Promise of Politics*, Humanitas, 2010) and Martin Ford (*Rise of the Robots*, Corint, 2019).
