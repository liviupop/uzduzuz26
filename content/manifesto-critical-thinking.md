Critical thinking has become a slogan, which is a problem, because slogans are exactly the kind of language that critical thinking is supposed to push back against. We try to use the term carefully.

By critical thinking we mean a discipline, not an attitude. The disciplined version requires a willingness to consider that your first response to a question is probably wrong, and to do the slower work of finding a better one. It is not the same as scepticism, which is suspicious of every claim by reflex, and it is not the same as cynicism, which is suspicious of every claim and pleased with itself for being so. Critical thinking, in the sense we mean, includes the patience to be persuaded.

The discipline applies first to one's own beliefs. It is comparatively easy to demand that other people's claims be subjected to scrutiny; the harder work is subjecting one's own. We try to organise our projects so that we are routinely confronted with evidence that might revise our positions, and so that we cannot dismiss it without consideration. This is one of the reasons we welcome partners who do not always agree with us.

The discipline applies, second, to the tools we use. We do not adopt a piece of software because everyone else has; we do not refuse it because refusal is fashionable. We try to ask, in each case, what the tool does to the work and to the people doing the work, and to revise our use accordingly. The same applies to methods, formats, and funding sources.

The discipline applies, third, to the language we use to describe our own activities. The European cultural sector has a vocabulary that is sometimes precise and sometimes inflated. We try to notice when our own writing has tipped into the inflated register and to bring it back down. The present sentence is, we hope, an example of trying.

Critical thinking in this sense is not an end in itself. It is a precondition for the activity that gives our work its texture, which is the willingness to follow questions into territories where we do not yet know what we will find. That posture has its own name in our list of principles.
