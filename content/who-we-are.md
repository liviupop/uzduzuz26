# Who we are

## In one paragraph

uzinaduzina is a non-profit cultural association founded in Cluj-Napoca in 2008 and rebooted in 2020. We work at the intersection of art, intangible heritage, education, and critical technology, in continuous dialogue with European partners across seven countries.

## In a few

uzinaduzina is a non-profit cultural association in Cluj-Napoca, Romania. Founded in 2008 by a small group of researchers and friends, we rebooted in 2020 with a sharpened focus: how cultural and educational institutions can hold their ground in an information environment increasingly hostile to slow thought.

Since then we have coordinated two major Erasmus+ KA220 partnerships totalling roughly half a million euros (*Green Genesis Startup*, *Drops of Sustainable Development*), led the *Goana după meteor* astronomy camp at Mociu, and hosted the *AI4NGOs* youth-worker training in Cluj for CCIF Cyprus. Our partners come from Cyprus, Italy, Greece, Spain, Croatia, and Poland.

What is specific to us: an explicit commitment to European cooperation as funding architecture; education for the whole person in the spirit of Comenius, Froebel, Montessori, and Korczak; and a continuous line of work on technology and democratic life. We work small on purpose. The dozen-person scale lets us coordinate by conversation rather than by command.

## The name

The name translates loosely as *a dozen work initiatives in a factory*. The original idea was to keep the organisation elastic enough that each of its founders could carry their own research and projects forward without one suffocating the other. The phrasing appears in our 2020 *democraicy* concept document:

> "Our name roughly translates as a dozen work initiatives in a factory, since we wanted this organisation to allow each of us to further our research and develop projects and events that would accord with our initiatives."

The 2020 document listed our areas of work: intangible cultural heritage, digital preservation of cultural artefacts, traditional crafts reinterpreted from an urban perspective, the evolution of public space in post-communist Romania, anthropological research, contemporary and outsider art, civic education and human rights.

The name has, in retrospect, an anthropological coherence we did not consciously design for. Robin Dunbar's research on primate brains and human group sizes identified several concentric layers of meaningful relationships a person can sustain cognitively. The innermost circle holds about five people; the next layer up, the *sympathy group* (those one would contact in a personal crisis, with whom one collaborates closely, whom one carries in active memory by name and situation), holds between ten and fifteen. A dozen is, in that sense, exactly the size of human group with which one can have relationships close enough to produce something coherent together. Below twelve, the group is too small to cover the diversity of competences a complex project requires; above fifteen, one starts to lose the ability to maintain meaningful relationships with each member. uzinaduzina, as a form of organisation, encodes this intuition in its name.

## What we have done

We coordinate and participate in projects funded through [**Erasmus+**](https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/) and [**AFCN**](https://www.afcn.ro/), and by the [**Polish Institute Romania**](https://institutulpolonez.ro/), [**Botnar Foundation**](https://www.fondationbotnar.org/) (through QUB / [**Centrul Cultural Clujean**](https://centrulculturalclujean.ro/)), and [**FDSC** (Fundația pentru Dezvoltarea Societății Civile)](https://www.fdsc.ro/). For the full project list with references, periods and budgets, see the [projects page](/projects).

Two major Erasmus+ KA220 projects coordinated as lead:

- [Green Genesis Startup](/projects/green-genesis-startup) (KA220-YOU, €205.144, 2022–2024). A consortium of five partners: CO-LABORY (Italy), CCIF (Cyprus), Innovation Hive (Greece), ACD La Hoya (Spain), Udruga Murtila (Croatia).
- [Drops of Sustainable Development](/projects/drops-sustainable-development) (KA220-ADU, €250.000, 2024–2026, ongoing).

Three of our own events organised in 2025 with exceptional feedback:

- [Goana după meteor](/projects/goana-dupa-meteor): astronomy camp in Mociu, August 2025, AFCN €35.547. 22 participants, 100% recommendation rate.
- [AI4NGOs](/projects/ai4ngos): three days on AI and democracy, August 2025, in collaboration with CCIF (€5.238). 5 participants, perfect 5.0/5 score across all 15 measurements.
- [Wooden Wonders](/projects/wooden-wonders): youth exchange on traditional woodworking crafts, September 2025, in consortium with 4 Elements (Croatia), €11.742. 13 participants, 9.08/10 satisfaction score.

Cumulatively in 2025, the feedback report collected 40 responses across the three events, with a satisfaction rate above 95% and 100% recommendation rate.
