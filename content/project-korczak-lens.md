Your story guided by Janusz Korczak's vision. A project carried out together with the Polish Institute in Bucharest, 2021–2022.

> The child does not become a man, he is already a man.
>
> — Janusz Korczak

With this famous statement, the paediatrician and educator Janusz Korczak changed the way we look at childhood. For more than thirty years he took care of thousands of poor and family-less children, Jewish, but not only.

## Korczak Lens · the workshop

A workshop based on Janusz Korczak's vision of partner relationships with children and the child's right to respect. Designed for educators and parents.

## The story of you · exploration guide

A seminar on scenarios for exploring children's rights, for educators or parents. Pedagogical sheet and illustrated guide by **Mălina Cristea**.

> Although there have always been children, in all historical times, we have not always seen them the same way. I want to share their story with you.

During the first civilisations, children had similar responsibilities to adults. They all contributed to finding food, gathering plants, participating in hunting, or in the process of making tools. In that early world there were few objects and they were all created from what individuals found in nature. With the emergence of great civilisations and, especially, the emergence of writing, the opportunity arose for children from wealthier families to go to school. School did not resemble what we know today, but they could explore mathematics, music, reading, and writing.
