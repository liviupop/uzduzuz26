An attempt to recover hidden stories of Sibiu. A city with a rich multi-ethnic history, of which we gather a series of ten lesser-known stories tied to specific places in town. The project invites visitors and locals into an unusual discovery adventure through an analog augmented exploration kit.

## what it is

Ten illustrations printed on transparent foil — the kind university teachers used for overhead projectors in the 1980s. Held at arm's length inside a small frame, each illustration overlays the urban landscape in front of you and tells a single short story. The effect is closer to old children's filmstrips than to digital AR: an analog augmentation, deliberately simple, deliberately tactile.

We avoid LiDAR, AR.js, and the rest of the contemporary AR vocabulary. Not because the technology lacks elegance, but because it widens social cleavages and tends, in its current form, to age badly. A what3words geocode is enough to get you to the right corner of the city. The rest is your hand, the foil, and a story.

## why it matters

Contemporary Sibiu reinvents itself as a tourist city, cultural and explicitly European. In that new context, doubled by a real-estate boom, the traces of minorities grow fainter and the interwoven histories of the place fray under the eyes of inattentive residents.

The project gathers stories from each community while keeping the route through the city organic: you walk where the kit takes you, and the kit takes you through Saxon, Romanian, Hungarian, Roma, and other layers of the same streets, often in the same square.

## a sample story · the houses speak

> *We see you! We see you!* From the Great Square, motionless, under sun or snow, the houses of Sibiu have watched the city without rest. *We are here, all around, and our eyes, ever open, have watched over the city and the lives of its people. Quietly, hidden under the plaster of our eyelids, we have kept the small stories of the past.*

The narrative cycle starts with St. John Nepomuk, the saint of secrets, who landed in the citadel around 1734 — a Habsburg saint not entirely welcome in a Saxon town. He moved three times. We follow him, then the Liars' Bridge (which is really the *lying* bridge — a translation error from Liegenbrücke, the *reclining* bridge). Then the Brukenthal College, six hundred years old, still upright, still teaching. Then the *purligari* — the porters from "pour la gare", carrying the merchants' bags to catch the train. Each station holds one short, careful piece of multi-ethnic memory, told by a house, signed by a community.

## what makes the project ours

Sibiu is reinventing itself, and many of its memory layers risk being smoothed out in the process. We retell the city through analog devices, not because we are nostalgic but because the cheap technology forces a slower kind of attention. The kit is durable, repairable, and works without batteries — three properties that turn out to matter when you want a thing to last.

## status

In development. The next steps:

- Production of the illustrated foils and frames.
- A web version, possibly a "digital diaprojector" with scrollable scene transitions, possibly a what3words-gated WebAR layer for those physically present.
- Translations into English and German, since Sibiu's audience is multilingual by tradition.
