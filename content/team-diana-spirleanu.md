Personal development counsellor and youth facilitator. Within uzinaduzina, Diana accompanies youth groups in international exchanges, with responsibility for group facilitation, participant wellbeing, and intercultural learning processes.

## role at uzinaduzina

Diana works closely with the young people who travel through uzinaduzina's mobility projects. Her role bridges two registers that are usually kept apart: the practical logistics of international exchange (programmes, schedules, group dynamics) and the emotional infrastructure that makes those exchanges genuinely formative (psychological safety, conflict resolution, reflective practice). She is the person in the room who notices when a group needs to slow down, when a participant is struggling, when a workshop is running on momentum rather than meaning.

## background

Diana has worked as a personal development counsellor and educational facilitator for more than fifteen years, with formative roles at Upaya (2009–2014), Retronew (2014–2023), the Sustainable Education Association (2020–present), and currently POLCREATIV (since 2025). Her practice draws on Nonviolent Communication (NVC), embodied and somatic methods, and experiential approaches to learning.

Between 2017 and 2020 she was co-founder and facilitator of the *School of Joy*, an Agile Learning Centre that offered non-formal learning processes for children and young people aged six to fifteen. The School of Joy was one of the first attempts in Romania to translate the international Agile Learning Centres model into local conditions, and it shaped Diana's commitment to learning environments that respect each child's pace and interests.

Her professional path is unusual. She trained as a pharmacist at the University of Medicine and Pharmacy in Cluj-Napoca and worked in the field for several years, including a research period in Copenhagen at Novo Nordisk and at Unikem. The transition from pharmacy to facilitation reflects a gradual move from caring for the body in clinical settings to working with people more holistically, integrating the somatic, the relational, and the cognitive.

She holds certifications as a Trainer (2025), Body-Oriented Coach from The Somatic School (2021), Personal Development Counsellor (2015), Children's Yoga Instructor (Dublin, 2014), and Trance Dance Facilitator (Lisbon, 2017).

## areas of expertise

- Group facilitation in non-formal education contexts
- Nonviolent Communication (NVC) and conflict resolution
- Somatic and embodied practices for young people and adults
- Wellbeing support during international youth exchanges
- Workshop design for socio-emotional skills development
- Counselling for personal and emotional development (individual and group)
- Working with educators, parents, and families

## where Diana works at uzinaduzina

Diana joins international youth exchanges that uzinaduzina organises or participates in. Her involvement is not tied to a single project but to a kind of work: any mobility, exchange, or workshop in which young people spend extended time together and need support beyond logistics.
